Federal Rules

  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC) rules on consumer protection and fair trade practices.
  • Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) rules on workplace safety.
  • Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) rules on environmental regulations and pollution control.
  • Food and Drug Administration (FDA) rules on food safety and drug approvals.
  • Federal Communications Commission (FCC) rules on telecommunications and broadcasting.
  • Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) rules on securities and financial market regulations.
  • Department of Transportation (DOT) rules on transportation safety and regulations.
  • Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) rules on immigration and border control.
  • Federal Reserve rules on monetary policy and banking regulations.
  • Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) rules on aviation safety and regulations.

State Rules

  • State labor laws governing minimum wage, working hours, and employment standards.
  • State-specific building codes and construction regulations.
  • State environmental regulations for land use, water resources, and waste management.
  • State-level vehicle registration and licensing rules.
  • State education regulations, including standards for curriculum and teacher certification.
  • State-specific rules for professional licensing and regulations (e.g., lawyers, doctors, nurses).
  • State tax laws and regulations, including income tax and sales tax.
  • State-level zoning and land-use regulations.
  • State-specific criminal laws and sentencing guidelines.
  • State-level regulations for alcohol sales and consumption.

Traffic Rules

  • Speed limit rules, specifying the maximum speed allowed on different types of roads.
  • Rules for stopping at traffic signals, including red, yellow, and green lights.
  • Yield rules, requiring drivers to give the right-of-way to other vehicles or pedestrians.
  • Rules for proper lane usage, including passing and merging.
  • Rules for using turn signals when changing lanes or making turns.
  • Parking rules, including restrictions on parking in designated areas or for specified durations.
  • Rules regarding driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Seat belt laws, requiring drivers and passengers to wear seat belts.
  • Rules for using headlights, including when to use high beams or low beams.
  • Rules for pedestrian safety, such as crosswalk usage and yielding to pedestrians.
  • Please note that the specific rules may vary by jurisdiction and are subject to change, so it's important to consult the relevant authorities or legal resources for the most up-to-date and accurate information.
